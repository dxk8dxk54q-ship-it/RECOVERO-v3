What to do if your car breaks down at night

Breaking down at night can be a frightening experience. Visibility is low, and you feel more vulnerable. Here is our expert guide to staying safe until a Recovero truck arrives.

1. Move to Safety Immediately

If your vehicle is still moving, try to pull over to a safe location as far from traffic as possible. If you are on a motorway, try to reach the hard shoulder or an emergency area. Always exit the vehicle through the doors away from the traffic (the passenger side).

2. Make Yourself Visible

Switch on your hazard lights immediately. If it's dark or foggy, keep your sidelights on as well. If you have a high-visibility jacket in your car (which we highly recommend keeping in the glovebox), put it on before exiting the vehicle.

Recovero Expert Tip:

Never stand behind or in front of your vehicle. If another car hits your vehicle, you could be pinned. Stand well back from the road, ideally behind a crash barrier.

3. Call for Professional Recovery

Once you are in a safe position, call our 24/7 dispatch line. Provide your exact location—using "What3Words" is incredibly helpful for our drivers to find you in the dark. Lock your car doors and wait in a safe, visible spot away from the flow of traffic.
